This case study analyzes customer purchase behavior for AeroFit treadmills to understand how different customer characteristics influence product selection. The dataset includes demographic details, income levels, fitness ratings, and usage patterns of customers who purchased one of three treadmill models.

The analysis focuses on identifying key factors that differentiate buyers of entry-level, mid-range, and premium treadmills. Through descriptive statistics, visual analysis, and probability concepts, the study uncovers how variables such as income, fitness level, and weekly usage impact purchasing decisions.

The results reveal distinct customer segments, where entry-level products attract budget-conscious beginners, while premium models are preferred by high-income and performance-oriented users. The study highlights that behavioral factors like fitness and usage are stronger predictors of product choice than demographic attributes like age.

Based on these insights, the case study provides actionable business recommendations, including targeted marketing strategies, personalized product recommendations, and customer upgrade pathways to improve sales and customer satisfaction.
